First of all, I want to do an statement, this is not completely my job, this is an adaptation of the first chapter of Michael Nielsen's book and that also uses knowledge of the Youtube videos done by 3Blue1Brown (here you can find the video playlist), but I tried to reflect what I learned in a written article.

The article is kind of divided in two main parts, one that explains the basics of the neural networks and the knowledge that it will later be implemented and used (such as gradient descent, stochastic gradient descent, backpropagations, weights and biases...), and the other part that explains how to apply it into Python and what are the results that we can expect from the project, how good will it be (although it can be improved as Michael Nielsen explains in the rest of his book).

Neural networks and deep learning are probably the future of the computer science

The python files needed to follow the article are all included (not only the main file, Network.py, but also the comparaison files that later are mentioned), and everything in them is commented so the reader can follow the code at the same time that reads the article and learns about neural networks.

Finally, the document is ended by a small conclusion about how the neural networks are a phenomenal recourse and that they may constitute the future of programming, but that it still has a great road ahead because of their multiple limitations and factors.
